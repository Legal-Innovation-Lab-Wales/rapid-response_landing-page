What does this do? Helping find info about parental leave


A little bit of background

Even in these early stages of this ever-expanding pandemic, the COVID-19 crisis has had a catastrophic impact on economies and societies around the world. In the UK, many retailers and manufacturers have been forced to close down for the foreseeable future with many thousands of workers facing an indefinite period of unemployment, with families to support and with bills to pay. Therefore, there are a great number of people in desperate need of legal advice and support in a plethora of fields including: employment law and housing law.

As a result of this unprecedented challenge, the Swansea Law Clinic, with the support of the Law School’s Legal Innovation Lab, is exploring ways of providing initial guidance and information to members of the public facing legal issues arising as a direct result of the impact of Coronavirus.

Legal Innovation Lab Wales is a ERDF-funded research and development facility located in the expanded Law School within the Mall Building. The project has secured funding until June 2023 and has 3 principal aims:

  • Drive the development of a robust and vibrant LegalTech sector in Wales, delivering economic impact and enabling growth in the Welsh legal services sector;
  • Support the development of toolkits that enhance the business of law and access to justice, delivering societal impacts, and;
  • Enable the development of technological platforms and frameworks to support counterterrorism, defence and security.

So what have we come up with?

After lengthy discussions, it was decided that one of the key priorities for struggling families is parental leave, as they contend with childcare responsibilities. This webpage includes information about unpaid parental leave via a “decision tree”. It provides details on who can apply for it and for which children. It also provides details on the procedure you should use to apply for it, how much unpaid leave you can apply for, how it affects your entitlement to welfare benefits and your other employment rights, as well as the circumstances under which your employer can restrict your parental leave rights. As well as the decision tree, it is also planned that a Frequently Asked Questions section will be added in due course.

It is recognised that there are many other areas of law that people may need help with, therefore this page may be expanded to other subject areas at a later date – so watch this space!
