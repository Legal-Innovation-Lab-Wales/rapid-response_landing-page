Discover if you are likely to be eligible for unpaid parental leave


A little bit of background

Even in these early stages of this ever-expanding pandemic, the COVID-19 crisis has had a catastrophic impact on economies and societies around the world. In the UK, many retailers and manufacturers have been forced to close down for the foreseeable future with many thousands of workers facing an indefinite period of unemployment, with families to support and with bills to pay. Therefore, there are a great number of people in desperate need of legal advice and support in a plethora of fields including: employment law and housing law.

As a result of this unprecedented challenge, the Swansea Law Clinic, with the support of the Law School’s Legal Innovation Lab, is exploring ways of providing initial guidance and information to members of the public facing legal issues arising as a direct result of the impact of Coronavirus.

Legal Innovation Lab Wales is a ERDF-funded research and development facility located in the expanded Law School within the Mall Building. The project has secured funding until June 2023 and has 3 principal aims:

  • Drive the development of a robust and vibrant LegalTech sector in Wales, delivering economic impact and enabling growth in the Welsh legal services sector;
  • Support the development of toolkits that enhance the business of law and access to justice, delivering societal impacts, and;
  • Enable the development of technological platforms and frameworks to support counterterrorism, defence and security.

So what have we come up with?

After lengthy discussions, it was decided that one of the key priorities for struggling families is parental leave, as they contend with childcare responsibilities. This webpage includes information about unpaid parental leave via a “decision tree”. It provides details on who can apply for it and for which children. It also provides details on the procedure you should use to apply for it, how much unpaid leave you can apply for, how it affects your entitlement to welfare benefits and your other employment rights, as well as the circumstances under which your employer can restrict your parental leave rights. As well as the decision tree, it is also planned that a Frequently Asked Questions section will be added in due course.

It is recognised that there are many other areas of law that people may need help with, therefore this page may be expanded to other subject areas at a later date – so watch this space!

Welsh Title

Canfod os ydych yn gymwys i dderbyn absenoldeb rhiant di-dâl.


Cefndir

Hyd yn oed yn ystod cymalau cynnar y pandemig sy’n enhangu’n barhaus, mae argyfwng COVID-19 wedi cael effaith trychinebus ar economïau a chymdeithasau ledled y byd. Yn y DU, mae llawer o fanwerthwyr a gweithgynhyrchwyr wedi’u gorfodi i gau am y dyfodol rhagweladwy, gyda miloedd o weithwyr yn wynebu cyfnod amhenodol o ddiweithdra, gyda theuluoedd i’w cefnogi a biliau i dalu. Felly, mae gwir angen cyngor a chymorth cyfreithiol ar nifer o bobl mewn amryw o feysydd, gan gynnwys: cyfraith cyflogaeth a chyfraith tai.

O ganlyniad i’r her ddigynsail hon, mae Clinig y Gyfraith Abertawe, gyda chefnogaeth Cyfraith Arloesi Cyfreithiol Ysgol y Gyfraith, yn archwilio ffyrdd o ddarparu arweiniad a gwybodaeth cychwynnol i aelodau o’r cyhoedd sy’n wynebu materion cyfreithiol sy’n codi o ganlyniad i effaith y Coronafeirws.

Mae Labordy Arloesi Cyfreithiol Cymru yn gyfleuster ymchwil a datblygu sy’n cael ei ariannu gan yr EDRF. Fe leolir yn Ysgol y Gyfraith sydd wedi ehangu i adeilad Mall y brifysgol. Mae’r prosiect wedi llwyddo sicrhau cyllid tan fis Mehefin 2023 ac mae ganddo 3 prif nod:

  • Hybu datblygiad o sector Technoleg Gyfreithiol cadarn a bywiog yng Nghymru, er mwyn ddarparu effaith economaidd a galluogi twf yn sector gwasanaethau cyfreithiol Cymru;
  • Cefnogi’r gwaith o ddatblygu pecynnau cymorth sy’n gwella busnes y gyfraith a mynediad at gyfiawnder, gan sicrhau effaith ar gymdeithas, a;
  • Galluogi datblygiad o lwyfannau a fframweithiau technolegol i gefnogi gwrthderfysgaeth, amddiffyn a diogelwch.

Beth ydym ni’n gwneud?:

Penderfynwyd mai un o’r blaenoriaethau allweddol ar gyfer teuluoedd sy’n cael trafferth yn ystod y cyfnod yma yw absenoldeb rhieni yn y gweithle, gan eu bod yn ymdopi â chyfrifoldebau gofal plant. Mae’r dudalen hon yn cynnwys gwybodaeth am absenoldeb rhiant di-dâl drwy ‘goeden benderfynu’. Mae’n rhoi manylion ynghylch pwy all wneud cais ac am ba blant. Mae hefyd yn rhoi manylion am y weithdrefn y dylech ei defnyddio i wneud cais, faint o absenoldeb di-dâl y gallwch ofyn amdano, sut mae’n effeithio are eich hawl i fudd-daliadau lles, a’ch hawliau cyflogaeth eraill. Yn ogystal, mae manylion â’r amgylchiadau lle gall eich cyflogwr gyfyngu are eich hawliau absenoldeb rhiant.

Yn ogystal â’r goeden benderfynu, mae yna fwriad y bydd adran cwestiynau cyffredin yn cael ei hychwanegu. Cydnabyddir bod llawer o feysydd eraill o’r gyfraith y bydd angen cymorth ar bobl, felly gobeithio bydd y dudalen hon yn cael ei diweddaru – bydd mwy o wybodaeth i ddilyn!
